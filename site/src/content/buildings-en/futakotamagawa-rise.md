## A Mixed-Use District at the Edge of the Tama River

On the north bank of the Tama River in Setagaya Ward's Tamagawa district, a phased large-scale development took shape between 2010 and 2015. Known as Futakotamagawa Rise, the project was led by Tokyu Corporation and Tokyu Land Corporation through the Futakotamagawa East District Urban Redevelopment Association. The development combines offices, residences, a hotel, retail, and a bus terminal in a cohesive mixed-use district connected directly to Futakotamagawa Station.

The main tower — Tower & Residence East — stands 42 floors above ground with a single basement level, reaching a height of 151.1 meters. Completed in 2010 as the centerpiece of the first phase, it anchors the district's skyline. The master plan was designed by London-based Conran and Partners, while the building-level design was handled by a Japanese joint venture of RIA, Tokyu Design Consultants, and Nihon Sekkei. A pedestrian promenade called the Ribbon Street runs from the station through the complex to Futakotamagawa Park and the Tama River, giving the district a clear spatial logic that visitors can trace on foot.

## From Urban Core to Riverside Green

One of the defining qualities of Futakotamagawa Rise is its deliberate transition from the concentrated activity of a busy station to the natural landscape of the Tama River. The adjacent Futakotamagawa Park covers approximately 6.3 hectares, and its integration with the development creates an unusual setting where a supertall tower and open riverbank coexist within a short walk of each other.

The development proceeded in stages: the first phase opened in 2010, an extension was added in 2011, and the final phase was completed in 2015. Several residential towers are included alongside the commercial and office components, creating a mixed district where living, working, and recreation overlap in a single walkable block. The project is frequently cited as a reference for large-scale suburban redevelopment in Tokyo — an existing stretch of low-rise city reconstituted as a riverside mixed-use district with a clear identity rooted in its relationship with the Tama River. Visitors looking for a hotel base to explore the area will find several options near the station, within easy walking distance of the complex.

## Summary

Futakotamagawa Rise reached full completion in 2015 in Tamagawa, Setagaya Ward, Tokyo. The main tower stands 42 floors and 151.1 meters tall, forming the vertical centerpiece of a large mixed-use district on the Tama River's north bank. Planned by Conran and Partners and realized by Tokyu, this development defined the contemporary character of Futakotamagawa — a place where the energy of a major station meets the calm of a broad river. Walking the Ribbon Street from the station to the water is the simplest way to feel the logic of that connection yourself.
