## A Mixed-Use Tower Born from the Roppongi 3-chome Redevelopment

The Sumitomo Fudosan Roppongi Grand Tower was completed in October 2016, rising 43 floors above ground and two below in the Roppongi 3-chome district of Minato City, Tokyo. At approximately 231 metres, it stands as the centrepiece of a large-scale urban redevelopment project led by the Roppongi 3-chome Eastern Area Urban Redevelopment Association and Sumitomo Realty & Development. The building was designed by Nikken Sekkei and constructed by a joint venture of Taisei Corporation and Obayashi Corporation. A direct underground connection to Roppongi-itchome Station on the Tokyo Metro Namboku Line defines the building's relationship to its urban context.

The lower and middle floors are given over to offices, while the upper storeys contain the "Roppongi Grand Tower Residence" — a residential component that stacks homes directly above workplaces. This vertical combination of uses within a single tower became a defining pattern of large-scale central-Tokyo development in the 2010s, and this building is a clear representative of that tendency.

## Roppongi-itchome and the Layered Hills of Minato City

The neighbourhood around Roppongi-itchome Station already carried a dense legacy of mixed-use redevelopment — Ark Hills, Izumi Garden Tower, and others had reshaped the area across earlier decades. The Sumitomo Fudosan Roppongi Grand Tower is a generation newer, joining the skyline as a landmark of the 2010s wave of private-sector urban renewal. Its 231-metre silhouette is visible from Roppongi-dori and Gaien Higashi-dori, making it one of the most prominent vertical markers on the western edge of Minato City.

Roppongi 6-chome, where Roppongi Hills stands, is only a wide street away. Walking from Roppongi-itchome Station and following the slope toward Azabudai, it becomes clear how closely successive redevelopment projects have reshaped this hilly corner of central Tokyo — and how much of the older neighbourhood texture still survives between them. The intersection of broad avenues toward Toranomon and the narrow lanes leading to Azabu is one of those rare places where Tokyo's layered urban logic is legible on foot.

## Summary

The Sumitomo Fudosan Roppongi Grand Tower was completed in 2016 with 43 above-ground floors and a height of approximately 231 metres, designed by Nikken Sekkei for the Roppongi 3-chome Eastern Area redevelopment. The tower's vertical mix of offices and residences, combined with its direct station connection, represents the mature formula of Tokyo's 2010s urban renewal. Start at Roppongi-itchome Station, walk through the Ark Hills precinct, and then look back at the tower's profile against the sky — the geography of successive redevelopment in this part of Minato City comes sharply into focus.
