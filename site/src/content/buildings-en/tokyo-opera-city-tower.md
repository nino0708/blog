## A tower of culture and offices in Hatsudai

Hatsudai, Nishi-Shinjuku, Shinjuku City, Tokyo. In 1996, a tower of 54 floors and a height of about 234 meters was completed. This is the Tokyo Opera City Tower. Together with the adjacent New National Theatre, it is the core skyscraper of a mixed-use development integrated with cultural facilities such as a concert hall and an art gallery.

By erecting a tower of this scale in Hatsudai, an area slightly west of the center of West Shinjuku, the reach of the Shinjuku subcenter extended toward Hatsudai. A composition that carries office functions while spreading cultural facilities at its feet defines the character of this building.

## Where a place to work and a place to listen overlap

What makes this tower distinctive is that, while being an office building, the district as a whole encloses places to enjoy music and art. Rather than a stand-alone business tower, a cultural hub and a workplace coexist within the same district.

On weekday afternoons, workers come and go; in the evenings and on weekends, people gather toward the hall — the overlapping flows of people at different times of day give Hatsudai a rhythm of its own.

## Summary

The Tokyo Opera City Tower is an office building completed in 1996, with 54 floors above ground, 4 below, and a height of about 234 meters. As the core of a mixed-use development integrated with cultural facilities, it has become the landmark of Hatsudai.

Look up at the sky in Hatsudai and this tower comes straight into view. From its feet, see for yourself a town where a place to work and a place for culture overlap.
